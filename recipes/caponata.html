Caponata siciliana

Description

The caponata siciliana used be a poor dish, but with time it has become a traditional dish of Sicilia. There are various versions of this dish, and most families have their own personal recipes, but they all share share a common base of fried eggplants. It can be serves hot or cold and it is delicious on toasted crusty bread.

Ingredients

  • Eggplants 1kg
  • White onions 250g
  • Green olives in brine (pitted) 200g
  • Pine nuts 50g
  • White wine vinegar 60g
  • Tomato paste 50g
  • Salt to taste
  • Celery 400g
  • Coppery tomatoes 200g
  • Desalted salted capers 50g
  • Sugar 60g
  • Basil to taste
  • Extravirgin olive oil to taste

Steps

  1. Clean the onion and slice it finely. Trim the celery and cut it into slices. Cut the green olives in half and remove the stone.
  2. Wash and dry the eggplants,trim them and cut it into pieces of about 2.5 cm. Do the same with tomatoes.
  3. Heat a pan and toast the pine nuts for a few minutes until they are golden.
  4. Put the oil in a high pan and heat it, add the eggplants and fry them until they are golden and browned. Dry them on a paper.
  5. In a large pan pour a generous drizzle of olive oil, heat it and then add the onion.Fry well until the onion is wilted, then add the celery.Let this brown well too and then add the capers, the olives, toasted pine nuts and tomatoes. Brown for a few moments, then cover with the lid and cook over low heat for 15-20 minutes.
  6. Prepare the sweet and sour sauce: in a small jug, pour the vinegar, tomato paste and the sugar. Mix well with a teaspoon and, once 15-20 minutes of cooking have passed, add salt and pour the sauce into the pan.
  7. Stir, raise the heat, and stir until the vinegar aroma has evaporated. Turn off the heat and add the fried eggplant and flavored with plenty of basil.
  8. Mix everything well.
