Spaghetti alla carbonara

Description

It is said that carbonara was first made in 1944 in Rome. A roman cook gathered the few ingredients available to the american soldiers, eggs, bacon and cheese, and turned them into the recipe we all know and love nowadays.

Ingredients

  • Olive oil
  • Guanciale
  • Spaghetti
  • Eggs
  • Cheese (pecorino romano)
  • Seasonings: salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Cook guanciale in oil until is brown and crispy.
  2. Boil the spaghetti in salted water, drain and cook them a little in the same pan where you have cooked guanciale (this adds some taste).
  3. Whisk the eggs, 1/2 of the cheese, and some pepper in a bowl until smooth.
  4. Pour the mixture over the pasta, stirring quickly, until creamy.
  5. Add some more cheese and pepper if you wish.
